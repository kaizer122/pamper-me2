# NoShowRisk on Client is the only platform-level signal that crosses Business boundaries

ADR-0006 establishes that ClientRelationship data never crosses Business boundaries. NoShowRisk is the single, deliberate exception: a platform-level score attached to the Client that aggregates no-show history across all Businesses, decays over time, and is surfaced to Businesses as a band (low/medium/high) plus a numeric score. The underlying Booking history that drives the score is never disclosed across Businesses — only the aggregate. We accept this exception because no-show prevention serves all Businesses' shared interest, and the alternative (each Business learning bad-actor patterns from scratch) makes the platform less valuable than the sum of its parts. Future cross-Business signals must be evaluated against the same bar: shared interest, aggregate-only, never raw history.
