# TVA model: Business has a TaxRegime; prices are TTC; TVA snapshot is frozen on Booking

Tunisian TVA is modeled on the Business via a `taxRegime` enum (`reel` | `forfaitaire`). ServiceOffering prices are stored and entered in TTC (TVA-included) — this matches Tunisian B2C convention and how Business owners think about prices. At Booking creation, the price is decomposed into HT + TVA components using the Business's regime and the platform-default TVA rate (19% for v1) and *frozen* on the Booking; subsequent Offering price changes do not affect already-created Bookings. Pamper Me's commission is computed on the gross and is independently TVA-able (Pamper Me operates as `regime reel`); settlement to the Business is `gross − commission_TTC`. Client receipts show only the service line (HT, TVA, TTC); commission TVA flows through a separate B2B invoice from Pamper Me to the Business each month, which `regime reel` Businesses use to deduct on their own TVA return. The `Money` type intentionally carries amount + currency only — never a TVA rate. TVA decomposition lives on the Booking and the receipt, not on the unit of money. We rejected putting `tvaRate` on Money because it bakes a Tunisian assumption into a primitive that should remain a unit of currency, and would couple every monetary value in the system to a tax model.
