# Walk-in and phone-channel Bookings can attach to unverified Clients; OTP requirement is marketplace-only

Bookings carry a `BookingChannel` (`marketplace` | `walk-in` | `phone` | `admin`). Walk-in and phone channels are Business-initiated: the salon adds the Client at the counter or after a phone call, without going through the Client-facing OTP flow. To support this, the Client model gains an `identityVerified: boolean` flag — true once OTP is complete, false for walk-in / phone / admin-created records. Phone is always required on a Client; OTP is conditional on channel. This refines ADR-0007: the "OTP at booking is non-negotiable" stance applies to the marketplace channel only. Unverified Clients have restricted privileges: cannot use online payment (Konnect requires verified identity), cannot post Reviews, are not enrolled in marketing email. When the same phone number later completes OTP via the marketplace, the records auto-merge — Booking history, NoShowRisk, ClientRelationships all carry over. NoShowRisk attaches to the Client record (keyed by phone) regardless of verification status, so walk-in no-shows accumulate risk just like marketplace no-shows. We rejected requiring OTP for walk-ins (would make the SaaS hostile at the counter) and rejected a separate "WalkInGuest" entity (would re-introduce the dual-entity merge model that ADR-0007 was specifically designed to avoid).
