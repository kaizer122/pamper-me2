# Client identity is platform-owned; per-Business data lives on a separate ClientRelationship

A Client is a single platform-wide entity (one human, one identity, one login). The first time a Client books with a Business, a ClientRelationship record is created on the Business side; this is where notes, allergies, color formulas, marketing consent, and per-Business booking history live. The platform has no read access to ClientRelationship content, and ClientRelationship data never crosses Business boundaries — Salon X cannot see Salon Y's notes about the same human. We rejected a single-table model (would leak commercial data across Businesses) and a per-Business identity model (forces clients to manage many accounts and breaks marketplace search/repeat booking). A future engineer may be tempted to denormalize notes onto Client; this is forbidden — it violates the privacy boundary that lets Businesses trust the platform with their commercial data.
