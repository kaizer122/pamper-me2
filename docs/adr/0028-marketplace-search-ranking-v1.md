# Marketplace search: availability is a hard filter; ranking is unpaid in v1

Marketplace search applies hard filters (ServiceType match, requested day/time availability, optional price range, optional Practitioner constraint) and then ranks the remaining Businesses by a weighted score: distance from the Client's chosen location, aggregate Business rating, recency of activity in the last 30 days, and profile completeness. **Availability is a hard filter, not a ranking signal** — Businesses with no slot in the requested window are excluded entirely, never deranked. This is the marketplace's core promise: every result is bookable. There are no paid promotion or sponsored slots in v1; we deliberately defer that monetization lever to avoid biasing the signal collection we need to tune the ranking. Cold-start handling for new Businesses (avoiding "0 reviews" sinking to the bottom) uses a soft floor — new Businesses surface in their distance band rather than being ranked below all rated competitors. The exact weight schedule is intentionally not committed in this ADR — it is expected to evolve from real-traffic data — but the *structure* (hard-filter availability, unpaid ranking, the listed signals) is the v1 commitment.
